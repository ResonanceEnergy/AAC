## THESIS

Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) represent the state's counter-offensive
in the monetary transition. Unlike stablecoins (Track 2) which extend private
sector influence, CBDCs reclaim and EXPAND government control over money flows.
They enable programmable money, real-time monetary policy, and — critically —
unprecedented financial surveillance. AAC must understand this track not just
for trading, but for systemic risk management and civil-liberty-aware positioning.

## 2. DESIGN MODELS

### Wholesale CBDC (Interbank Only)
- Used for interbank settlement between central bank and commercial banks
- Replaces/enhances existing RTGS systems
- **NOT consumer-facing** — most conservative approach
- Examples: Bank of France experiments, Singapore's Project Ubin
- **AAC Impact**: Minimal direct trading impact; speeds institutional settlement

### Retail CBDC (Consumer-Facing)
- Direct central bank-to-citizen digital currency
- Replaces or supplements physical cash
- Two sub-models:
  - **Account-based**: Each citizen has an account with the central bank
  - **Token-based**: Digital bearer instrument (closer to digital cash)
- **AAC Impact**: MASSIVE — changes how money enters/exits markets

### Hybrid CBDC
- Central bank issues the currency, commercial banks distribute it
- Most likely model for developed economies (EU, UK, US)
- Preserves bank intermediation while gaining digital speed
- **AAC Impact**: Moderate — existing bank relationships preserved

## 3. PROGRAMMABLE MONEY: OPPORTUNITY AND THREAT

### What Programmable Money Enables
Central banks can encode rules directly into currency:

1. **Expiration dates**: Money that must be spent before a deadline (stimulus spending)
2. **Spending restrictions**: Money that cannot be used for certain purchases
3. **Geographic limits**: Money valid only in certain regions
4. **Tiered interest rates**: Different rates for different balance levels
5. **Negative interest rates**: Impossible with cash, trivial with CBDCs
6. **Conditional transfers**: Welfare payments locked to specific categories
7. **Speed of monetary policy**: Instant transmission vs months/years of lag

### The Social Credit Dimension
China's Social Credit System demonstrates the convergence path:
- e-CNY digital yuan + social credit scoring = behavioral monetary control
- Low-score citizens face: restricted travel, loan denial, reduced services
- High-score citizens receive: preferential rates, access to premium services
- **This is not speculation — it is deployed and operational in China**

### Why This Matters for AAC
- **Risk**: Governments can freeze CBDC holdings instantly (no court order needed)
- **Risk**: Programmable restrictions could block crypto purchases via CBDC
- **Risk**: Capital controls become frictionless to enforce
- **Opportunity**: New monetary policy instruments create predictable market moves
- **Opportunity**: CBDC launches create temporary dislocations and arbitrage windows

## 4. MAJOR CBDC PROGRAMS

### Digital Euro (ECB)
- **Status**: Investigation phase completed October 2023, preparation phase active
- **Timeline**: Earliest launch ~2028
- **Design**: Hybrid model, privacy-preserving for small transactions
- **Cap**: Likely €3,000 holding limit per citizen
- **ECB Position**: "Not a replacement for cash, but a complement"
- **Reality**: Cash usage in eurozone dropped 50% since 2019
- **AAC Watch**: EUR/USD impact, bank deposit outflow risk, stablecoin regulatory response

### Digital Yuan (e-CNY)
- **Status**: Largest CBDC pilot globally, 260M+ wallets opened
- **Design**: Two-tier (PBoC issues, banks distribute), controllable anonymity
- **Transaction Volume**: $250B+ in pilot transactions
- **Integration**: Alipay, WeChat Pay accepting e-CNY
- **Belt & Road**: Potential for bilateral trade settlement (bypassing USD)
- **AAC Watch**: USD hegemony threat, capital flight signals, cross-border settlement data

### FedNow (US — Not a CBDC but related)
- **Status**: Launched July 2023 — instant 24/7 payment settlement
- **Design**: Not a CBDC, but builds the infrastructure a CBDC would require
- **Political Divide**: Republicans generally oppose US CBDC (privacy concerns),
  Democrats more open
- **Trump Admin Position (March 2025)**: Crypto strategic reserve, no CBDC push
- **AAC Watch**: Policy reversal signals, FedNow adoption metrics as proxy

### India e-Rupee
- **Status**: Pilot since December 2022, retail and wholesale tracks
- **Design**: Token-based, offline capability planned
- **Scale**: 4M+ users in pilot
- **Significance**: 1.4B population, massive remittance market
- **AAC Watch**: Remittance corridor disruption (currently $100B/year), XRP impact

## 5. CBDC vs. STABLECOIN: THE BATTLE FOR DIGITAL MONEY RAILS

### Fundamental Tension

| Dimension | CBDC | Stablecoin |
|---|---|---|
| **Issuer** | Central bank (sovereign) | Private company |
| **Backing** | Full faith of nation | Reserves (Treasuries, cash) |
| **Privacy** | Minimal to controllable | Pseudonymous to KYC'd |
| **Programmability** | Full (government decides) | Limited (issuer decides) |
| **Censorship** | Easy (sovereign power) | Medium (issuer can freeze) |
| **Cross-border** | Bilateral agreements needed | Permissionless |
| **Adoption driver** | Mandate/incentive | Market demand |
| **Settlement** | Instant (by design) | Near-instant (blockchain) |
| **Interest bearing** | By policy | Prohibited in US/EU |

### Likely Coexistence Scenarios

**Scenario A: Stablecoins Win (US-aligned world)**
- GENIUS Act empowers stablecoin issuance
- USD hegemony extends via USDT/USDC
- CBDCs stall in democratic countries due to privacy concerns
- **Probability**: 35%
- **AAC Position**: Heavy stablecoin yield farming, light CBDC preparation

**Scenario B: CBDCs Win (China-aligned world)**
- e-CNY succeeds in Belt & Road trade settlement
- EU launches digital euro with mandatory acceptance
- Stablecoins regulated into compliance or banned
- **Probability**: 25%
- **AAC Position**: Reduce stablecoin exposure, prepare for regulated channels

**Scenario C: Dual Track (Most Likely)**
- CBDCs for official channels, stablecoins for DeFi/crypto
- Most countries adopt hybrid approach
- Regulatory clarity creates clear separation of use cases
- **Probability**: 40%
- **AAC Position**: Diversified across both tracks, arbitrage the boundary

## 7. TRADING STRATEGIES

### Strategy 1: CBDC Launch Window Trading
- Every CBDC pilot/launch creates market dislocations
- Historical pattern: local crypto volumes spike before CBDC launch announcements
- Citizens front-run restrictions by moving to crypto
- **Entry**: Position in local crypto markets 3-6 months before announced pilot
- **Exit**: Sell into the launch-day volatility

### Strategy 2: CBDC Interest Rate Arbitrage
- CBDCs may carry different interest rates than bank deposits
- Exploit the spread between CBDC yield and alternative yield sources
- **Entry**: When CBDC rates deviate significantly from market rates
- **Exit**: When spreads normalize or policy changes

### Strategy 3: Capital Flight Detection
- Monitor on-chain stablecoin flows FROM countries implementing restrictive CBDCs
- Large minting of USDT/USDC in countries with strict capital controls = signal
- **Integration**: CryptoIntelligence whale_tracking + on-chain analysis modules

### Strategy 4: Privacy Premium Trading
- Privacy-preserving assets (Monero, Zcash, privacy L2s) appreciate when
  CBDC surveillance narratives gain traction
- Trade the narrative cycle, not the technology adoption
- **Trigger**: CBDC privacy controversy in major jurisdiction
- **Caution**: Regulatory risk on privacy coins is extreme

### Strategy 5: Cross-CBDC FX Arbitrage
- As multiple CBDCs launch, FX markets between them will be thin and inefficient
- Especially bilateral settlement corridors (e-CNY ↔ e-Rupee, etc.)
- **Timeline**: 2027-2030 as multiple CBDCs reach production
- **Preparation**: Build monitoring for CBDC FX rate feeds now
