## 1. THE BROKEN MONEY THESIS (Foundation)

### Core Problem: The Settlement-Transaction Disequilibrium
From Lyn Alden's *Broken Money* (2023, 100,000+ copies sold, 10 languages):

- **160+ currencies** exist worldwide, each with local monopoly jurisdiction
- Most currencies **rapidly devalue** over time with little acceptance outside borders
- **Transactions are orders of magnitude faster than settlements** — this gap creates
  centralized, corrupted money clusters
- The global financial system is effectively a **barter system** across borders
- Being born in the "wrong" country makes saving money harder than it needs to be
- **Technology drives money forward permanently and globally** — politics only temporarily and locally

### The Disequilibrium Model
```
TRANSACTION SPEED:  ████████████████████████████████  (milliseconds)
SETTLEMENT SPEED:   ████                              (days to weeks)
                    ↕ THIS GAP = centralization + corruption
```

### Historical Arc: Shells → Gold → Paper → Telegraph → Central Banks → Bitcoin
- Each technological leap reshaped money fundamentally
- Current era: **digital transactions** running on **analog settlement rails**
- The resolution: open-source money (Bitcoin), private digital money (stablecoins),
  government digital money (CBDCs) — all competing simultaneously

### Key Insight for AAC
The transition is NOT about picking one winner. It's about understanding
that **ALL THREE TRACKS** (decentralized, private, sovereign) will coexist
and create massive arbitrage, volatility, and structural opportunities.

## 2. THE THREE TRACKS OF MONETARY EVOLUTION

### Track 1: Decentralized Sound Money (Bitcoin, XRP, Layer 1s)
- **Bitcoin**: Digital gold, store of value, 21M supply cap, proof of work
- **XRP/XRPL**: Real-time gross settlement, 3-5 second consensus, cross-border payments
  - Named to US Crypto Strategic Reserve (March 2025, Trump executive order)
  - SEC ruling (July 2023): XRP token itself is NOT a security
  - Ripple Labs ≠ XRPL (separate governance)
- **Ethereum**: Smart contract platform, DeFi backbone, proof of stake
- **Solana**: High-throughput transactions, growing institutional adoption
- **Flare Network**: "The blockchain for data" — EVM-compatible L1
  - Enshrined data protocols (FTSO price feeds, FDC external events)
  - FAssets: XRPfi, BTCfi, DOGEfi — bringing smart contracts to non-smart-contract chains
  - 78% FLR staked (98% community), 1.7s block time, single-slot finality
  - $100M XRP institutional yield partnership (VivoPower, June 2025)
  - Flare 2.0 Vision: expanding consensus across chains

### Track 2: Private Digital Money (Stablecoins)
- **Market size**: $316 billion market cap (October 2025), $156B daily volume
- **Dominance**: 95% fiat-backed, 97% USD-denominated
- **Major players**:
  - **USDT (Tether)**: Largest by market cap, never fully audited, counterparty risk
  - **USDC (Circle)**: Fully regulated, transparent reserves, IPO trajectory
  - **RLUSD (Ripple)**: Ripple's regulated stablecoin for institutional use
  - **EURC**: Euro-denominated stablecoin (Circle), $225M market cap
- **Cross-border flows**: $1.4 trillion in 2024 (IMF data)
- **US GENIUS Act** (signed July 2025): Banks can now issue stablecoins
  - Stablecoins increasing demand for US Treasuries
  - Fed Governor Miran: "contributing to the dollar's dominance"
  - BIS estimate: lowers 3-month T-bill yields by 2-2.5 basis points
- **Erosion of sovereignty**: ECB, Bank of France, South African Reserve Bank all warn
  USD stablecoins threaten monetary sovereignty of non-US nations
- **First state-backed stablecoin**: Wyoming Frontier Stable Token (January 2026)

### Track 3: Sovereign Digital Money (CBDCs)
- CBDCs = direct claim on central bank (M0), stablecoins = private entity (M2)
- **Digital Euro**: ECB "strategic priority" but not legislated as of August 2025
- **Digital Yuan (e-CNY)**: Most advanced major CBDC, pilot in multiple Chinese cities
- **Social credit integration risk**: CBDCs enable programmable money + surveillance
- **77 countries** exploring CBDCs as of 2025
- AAC must monitor: which countries launch, adoption rates, restrictions imposed

## 4. RISK DOMAINS AAC MUST TRACK

### 4.1 De-Pegging Risk
- TerraUSD collapse (May 2022): $45B wiped out in one week
- USDC de-pegged during 2023 US banking crisis (SVB, Signature, Silvergate)
- **AAC Action**: Real-time stablecoin peg monitoring, automatic position exits on deviation

### 4.2 Counterparty Risk
- Tether: never completed a full audit (as of Feb 2026)
- Reserve concentration in single custodians = bank run vulnerability
- **AAC Action**: Diversify stablecoin exposure, weight toward audited issuers

### 4.3 Quantum Computing Threat
- IMF's Hélène Rey: quantum computers may break public-key cryptography
- Timeline uncertain but could cause catastrophic network failures
- **AAC Action**: Monitor quantum computing progress, hedge positions accordingly

### 4.4 Regulatory Shock Risk
- China banned stablecoins entirely
- CSRC banned Renminbi-denominated stablecoins (Feb 2026)
- Any major jurisdiction ban = market-moving event
- **AAC Action**: Regulatory event detection → automatic risk reduction

### 4.5 Sanctions & Illicit Use
- FATF reports stablecoins increasingly used for money laundering, terrorism financing
- North Korea, Iran, Russia using USDT to evade sanctions
- Increased regulation = potential exchange delistings, liquidity shocks
- **AAC Action**: Compliance-first approach, avoid flagged venues/tokens
