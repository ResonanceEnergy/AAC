## 1. BRICS & De-Dollarization

### 1.1 BRICS Evolution

**History**:
- **"BRIC" coined**: 2001 by Goldman Sachs economist Jim O'Neill
- **Formal founding**: 2009 (Russia arranged first summit in Yekaterinburg)
- **BRICS**: South Africa joined 2010 (added "S")
- **2024 Expansion**: Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE joined (Saudi Arabia invited but deferred)
- **2025**: Indonesia joined January 2025
- **Current Members**: 10 nations (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE, Indonesia)

**Economic Weight**:
- **Population**: 46% of world total (~3.7 billion people)
- **GDP (PPP)**: 35.6% of global GDP
- **Trade Volume**: Significant and growing — intra-BRICS trade increasingly settled in local currencies

### 1.2 BRICS Financial Architecture

**New Development Bank (NDB)**:
- **Capital**: $50B authorized capital
- **Approved Loans**: $32B+ across member nations
- **Currency**: Increasing share of loans in local currencies (not USD)
- **Significance**: Alternative to World Bank for developing nations
- **New Members**: Bangladesh, UAE, Uruguay, Egypt joined NDB

**Contingent Reserve Arrangement (CRA)**:
- **Size**: $100B liquidity pool
- **Purpose**: Balance-of-payments support (alternative to IMF)
- **Limitation**: Still partially tied to IMF conditionality for large drawdowns

### 1.3 De-Dollarization Mechanisms

**Alternative Payment Systems**:
| Country | System | Status |
|---|---|---|
| China | CIPS (Cross-border Interbank Payment System) | 1,500+ institutions, 182 countries |
| India | SFMS (Structured Financial Messaging System) | Domestic, expanding internationally |
| Russia | SPFS (System for Transfer of Financial Messages) | 550+ users, post-sanctions growth |
| Brazil | Pix (instant payments) | 150M+ users domestically |

**BRICS Bridge** (CBDC-based):
- Multi-CBDC platform for cross-border settlement
- Distinct from BIS mBridge project
- Enables BRICS nations to settle trade without USD
- Status: Conceptual/early development stage

**Currency Proposals**:
- **"R5"**: Proposed common currency named after members' currencies starting with R (Real, Ruble, Rupee, Renminbi, Rand)
- **"UNIT"**: Alternative proposal — backed 40% gold + 60% basket of BRICS currencies
- **Reality Check**: Economists largely view common currency as politically unlikely near-term (requires sacrifice of monetary policy independence)

### 1.4 Political Dynamics
- **Trump's Response** (February 2025): Threatened 100% tariffs on any nation that de-dollarizes, declared "BRICS is dead"
- **17th Summit**: Scheduled July 2025 in Rio de Janeiro (Brazil hosts)
- **Internal Tensions**: India-China border disputes, Iran sanctions complexity, Brazil's balancing act between US and BRICS

### 1.5 USD Reserve Currency Status

**Dollar Share of Global Reserves**:
| Year | USD Share | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| 2000 | 71% | — |
| 2010 | 62% | Declining |
| 2020 | 59% | Declining |
| 2024 | 57% | Declining but dominant |

- **Key Insight**: Dollar share declining slowly but NO replacement emerging — fragmentation instead
- **Gold**: Central banks buying at record pace (1,000+ tonnes/year 2022-2025)
- **CNY**: Only ~2.5% of reserves — China's capital controls limit adoption

**AAC Integration Signal**: CRITICAL. De-dollarization is a multi-decade macro theme. AAC must:
1. Monitor USD reserve share as leading indicator
2. Track BRICS trade settlement currency distribution
3. Gold allocation should hedge against accelerated de-dollarization
4. Stablecoin strategy (USDC/USDT) inherently tied to USD — diversification risk

- **SWIFT Bypass**: mBridge theoretically enables settlement without touching USD or SWIFT

**AAC Integration Signal**: HIGH. mBridge represents the institutional vanguard of multi-CBDC settlement. If successful, it fundamentally changes cross-border payment routing — impacting stablecoin demand.

## 3. IMF Special Drawing Rights (SDR/XDR)

### Overview
- **Created**: 1969 by International Monetary Fund
- **ISO 4217 Code**: XDR
- **Nature**: International reserve asset, NOT a currency — must be exchanged for member currencies

### Current Basket (2022-2027 review period)
| Currency | Weight | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| US Dollar (USD) | 43.38% | Reduced from 41.73% in 2016 |
| Euro (EUR) | 29.31% | Stable |
| Chinese Renminbi (CNY) | 12.28% | Added 2016, increased from 10.92% |
| Japanese Yen (JPY) | 7.59% | Reduced |
| British Pound (GBP) | 7.44% | Reduced |

### Key Events
- **Largest Allocation**: $650B (SDR 456.5 billion) in August 2021 — COVID-19 economic response
- **Total Outstanding**: ~SDR 943 billion
- **Zhou Xiaochuan Speech** (2009): PBoC Governor called for SDR to replace USD as global reserve currency — became foundational text for de-dollarization movement
- **US Veto Power**: US holds 16.7% voting share — effective veto on new SDR allocations (requires 85% supermajority)

### Strategic Significance
- SDR is the closest thing to a "world currency" that exists institutionally
- China has repeatedly promoted XDR as neutral alternative to dollar hegemony
- Syria pegs its pound to SDR (unusual case)
- IMF itself calls the SDR's current role "insignificant" relative to its potential
- Unit of account for: Universal Postal Union, Asian Development Bank, BIS

**AAC Integration Signal**: LOW-MEDIUM. SDR is a macro indicator, not a tradable asset. Track SDR basket composition changes as signal for currency power shifts. Next review in 2027 could increase CNY weight.

## 4. Post-Quantum Cryptography & Crypto Asset Risk

### The Quantum Threat to Cryptography

**Vulnerable Algorithms** (used in crypto):
| Algorithm | Use in Crypto | Quantum Attack | Timeline Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| ECDSA (secp256k1) | Bitcoin, Ethereum signatures | Shor's algorithm | Medium-term |
| RSA-2048 | TLS, certificates | Shor's algorithm | Medium-term |
| SHA-256 | Bitcoin mining (hash) | Grover's algorithm (√ speedup) | Low risk |
| AES-256 | Symmetric encryption | Grover's (effectively AES-128) | Low risk |

**Key Concepts**:
- **Mosca's Theorem**: If `X + Y > Z` (where X = shelf life of data, Y = migration time, Z = time until quantum threat), you must start migrating NOW
- **"Harvest Now, Decrypt Later"**: Adversaries record encrypted data today, plan to decrypt with future quantum computers
- **Impact on Bitcoin**: ECDSA signatures vulnerable IF quantum computers reach ~2,500+ logical qubits. Reused addresses most at risk (public key exposed). Addresses with unspent, never-revealed public keys are safer.

### NIST Post-Quantum Standards (Finalized August 2024)

| Standard | Based On | Type | Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FIPS 203** (ML-KEM) | CRYSTALS-Kyber | Key encapsulation | Lattice-based |
| **FIPS 204** (ML-DSA) | CRYSTALS-Dilithium | Digital signatures | Lattice-based |
| **FIPS 205** (SLH-DSA) | SPHINCS+ | Digital signatures | Hash-based |
| **FIPS 206** (FN-DSA) | Falcon | Digital signatures | Lattice-based (pending) |

### Six PQC Approaches
1. **Lattice-based** ✅ (NIST winners): Most efficient, well-studied — basis of ML-KEM and ML-DSA
2. **Hash-based** ✅ (NIST winner): Conservative, well-understood — SPHINCS+ (SLH-DSA)
3. **Code-based**: McEliece — very large keys but decades of cryptanalysis
4. **Multivariate**: Polynomial systems — some candidates broken
5. **Isogeny-based** ❌: SIDH/SIKE spectacularly broken in 2022 (by non-quantum attack)
6. **Symmetric-key**: AES-256, SHA-384 — already adequate with doubled key lengths

### Hybrid Cryptography (Transitional)
- **Apple PQ3** (iMessage): Hybrid classical + post-quantum key exchange (2024)
- **Signal PQXDH**: Post-quantum extended Diffie-Hellman (2023)
- **Open Quantum Safe (OQS)**: Open-source project (liboqs) implementing PQC algorithms
- **EU Roadmap**: Coordinated Implementation Roadmap published June 2025

### Implications for Crypto Assets
- **Bitcoin**: Could implement PQC signatures via soft fork (BIP); community discussion ongoing
- **Ethereum**: EIP for PQC account abstraction being researched
- **Timeline**: Most experts estimate 2030-2040+ for cryptographically-relevant quantum computers
- **Urgency**: Migration planning should begin NOW due to Mosca's theorem (multi-year migration cycles)

**AAC Integration Signal**: HIGH (LONG-TERM). Quantum risk is not imminent but preparation is essential. AAC should:
1. Monitor NIST PQC adoption timeline
2. Track Bitcoin/Ethereum PQC upgrade proposals
3. Prefer assets with clear PQC migration paths
4. Factor quantum risk into 10+ year asset allocation models

- **Drivers**: Central bank buying, geopolitical tensions (Israel-Iran, Russia-Ukraine), de-dollarization
- **"Gold Fatigue"**: By late 2025, some rotation from gold to platinum (up 44% in 2025)

### Historical Context
| Era | Gold Role | Key Event |
|---|---|---|

| Nixon Shock (1971) | Float begins | US unilaterally ended gold convertibility |

| ETF Era (2004+) | Financialized | SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) = 2nd largest ETF |
| Central bank buying (2022+) | Record purchases | 1,000+ tonnes/year (China, India, Turkey leading) |

### Gold-Bitcoin Relationship
- **"Digital Gold" Narrative**: Bitcoin positioned as gold 2.0 — scarce, portable, divisible
- **Correlation**: Low-to-moderate positive correlation, increasing during macro stress
- **Divergence**: Gold outperformed Bitcoin during traditional banking crises; Bitcoin outperformed during crypto bull markets
- **ISO 4217**: Gold = XAU; Bitcoin has no ISO code (but BTC is de facto standard)
- **China**: Top gold consumer since 2013, also among top Bitcoin mining nations (despite ban)

## 7. Global Debt & Fiscal Dynamics

### Current State
- **Global Debt**: $315T+ (2025 estimates, all sectors combined)
- **US National Debt**: $36T+ (2025)
- **US Debt-to-GDP**: ~125%+
- **Japan**: ~260% debt-to-GDP (highest among major economies)
- **China**: ~300% total debt-to-GDP (including corporate and household)

### Fiscal Dominance Thesis
- **Concept**: When government debt is so large that fiscal needs dominate monetary policy
- **Mechanism**: Central banks can't raise rates sufficiently because interest expense would become unsustainable
- **US Interest Expense**: $1T+ annually (2025) — exceeding defense budget
- **Implication**: Financial repression (keeping real rates negative) becomes the default policy — structurally bullish for hard assets (gold, BTC)

### Debt Spiral Indicators
1. **Interest/Revenue Ratio**: When interest payments exceed 15-20% of government revenue (US approaching this)
2. **Primary Deficit**: Government spending minus interest exceeds revenue
3. **Bond Market Vigilantes**: Rising term premium on long-term bonds (10Y, 30Y)
4. **Currency Weakness**: Persistent depreciation against hard assets

**AAC Integration Signal**: CRITICAL. The global debt trajectory is the MACRO THESIS underlying FFD's Broken Money framework. When debt becomes unserviceable → currency debasement → hard asset appreciation. This is the fundamental case for Bitcoin, gold, and the entire AAC strategy.

## 9. MiCAR — EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation

### Overview
- **Full Name**: Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114)
- **Effective Dates**: Titles III/IV (stablecoin rules) June 30, 2023; Full regulation December 30, 2024
- **Scope**: All EU member states (directly applicable, no national transposition needed)

### Key Provisions
| Area | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **CASPs** | Crypto-Asset Service Providers must be licensed in at least one EU member state |
| **EMT** | Electronic Money Tokens (stablecoins pegged to single currency): Must have e-money license, 1:1 reserves |
| **ART** | Asset Referenced Tokens (multi-asset stablecoins): Stricter requirements, ECB oversight for significant ARTs |
| **Whitepaper** | All crypto-asset issuers must publish a whitepaper (liability for misleading content) |
| **Market Abuse** | Insider trading and market manipulation rules applied to crypto |
| **Consumer Protection** | Right of withdrawal (14 days for public offers), risk disclosures |

### Significant Stablecoin Rules
- **Volume Cap**: Non-euro stablecoins (including USDT) limited to 200M transactions/day and €1M daily volume in EU
- **Impact on Tether**: USDT partially delisted from EU exchanges (Coinbase EU removed USDT December 2024)
- **USDC Advantage**: Circle obtained MiCA license, making USDC the "compliant" stablecoin in EU
- **Passporting**: CASPs licensed in one EU country can operate across all 27 member states

### Impact Assessment
- **Winners**: Regulated exchanges (Coinbase, Bitstamp), USDC, licensed CASPs
- **Losers**: Unregulated exchanges, USDT (volume cap), anonymous DeFi protocols
- **DeFi Exemption**: "Fully decentralized" protocols currently exempt — but definition is contentious

**AAC Integration Signal**: HIGH. MiCAR is the most comprehensive crypto regulation globally. AAC must be MiCAR-aware if operating with EU users/counterparties. USDC preference reinforced by MiCAR compliance.
